
1) へ

When a verb indicates movement to a certain place, the particle へ is put after the place to show the direction of the move.

きょうとへ　いきます。 I will go to Kyoto.
#
2) どこ[へ]も, なにも, だれも

When an interrogative takes the particle も and the verb following it is negative, all that is represented by the interrogative is denied.

どこ[へ]も　いきません。 I don't go anywhere.

なにも　たべません。I don't eat anything.

だれも　いません。Nobody is there.
#
3) いつ

To ask about time, なんじ(what time), なんようび(what day of the week), なんげつ(what month) or なんにち(which day of the month) are used. Other than these, the interrogative いつ(when) is also used to ask when something will happen/happened.

いつ　にほんへ　きましたか。 When did you come to Japan?
#
4) よ

よ is placed at the end of a sentence. It is used to emphasize information which the listener does not know, or to show that you are giving your judgement or views assertively.

この　でんしゃは　きょうとへ　いきますか。Does this train go to Koshien?

いいえ、いきません。つぎの　でんしゃ　ですよ。No, it doesn't. The next local train does.

ベーコンは　すごいですよ。 Bacon is awesome.
#
