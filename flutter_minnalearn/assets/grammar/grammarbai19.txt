
1) た-form

The た-form is made by changing て and で of the て-form into た and だ respectively.
#
2) た-form ことが　あります have the experience of V-ing

This sentence pattern is used to describe what one has experienced in the past. This is basically the same sentence as わたしは　Nが　あります which you have learned in Lesson 9. The content of one's experience is expressed by the nominalized phrase た-form こと.

うまに　のった　ことが　あります。 I have ridden a horse.

Note that it is, therefore, different from a sentence which merely states the fact that one did something at a certain time in the past.

きょねん　ほっかいどで　うまに　のりました。 I rode a horse in Hokkaido last year.
#
3) [た-form り, た-form り します] V, V, and so on

You learned an expression for referring to a few things and persons among many ( ~や ~ [など] ) in Lesson 10. The sentences learned here are used in referring to some actions among many other actions. The tense of this sentence pattern is shown at the end of the sentence.

にちようびは　テニスを　したり、えいがを　みたり　します。

On Sundays I play tennis, see a movie and so on.

せんしゅうの　にちようびは　テニスを　したり、えいがを　みたり　しました。

Last Sunday I played tennis, saw a movie and so on.

[Note] Make sure that you don't confuse the meaning of this sentence pattern with that of the て-form sentence which you learned in Lesson 16.

せんしゅうの　にちようびは　テニスをして、えいがを　みました。

Last Sunday I played tennis and then saw a movie.

In this pattern seeing a movie took place after playing tennis. In the り-pattern there is no time relation between the two activities. These activities are mentioned as example activities among the activities done on Sunday to imply that one did other activities besides them.
#
4) なります

なります means "become" and indicates changes in a state or condition.

さむい -> なむく　なります get cold

げんき[な] -> げんきに　なります get well

 ２５さい -> ２５さいに　なります become 25 years old
#
5) そうですね

そうですね is used when you agree or sympathize with what your partner in conversation said.

さむく　なりましたね。 It's got cold, hasn't it?

そうですね。 ···Yes,it has.
