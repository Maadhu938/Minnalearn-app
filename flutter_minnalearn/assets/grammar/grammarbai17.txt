
1) ない-form

The ない-form is formed in 3 ways:

Group I

In the verbs of this group the last sound of the ます-form is in the い-line. Replace it with the corresponding sound of the あ-line to make ない-form.

かきます → かかない

よみます → よまない

とります → とらない

The exceptions to this rule are such verbs as かいます, あいます etc. Instead of あ, insert わ.

Group II

The ない-form of verbs of this group is just the same as the ます-form.

たべます → たべない

みます → みない

Group III

The ない-form of します is the same as the ます-form. きます becomes こない.

べんきょうします→ べんきょうしない

します → しない

きます → こない

2) [V-ないで] Please don't...

This expression is used to ask or instruct someone not to do something.

げんき　ですから、しんぱいしないで　ください。

I am fine, so please don't worry about me.

ここで　しゃしんを　とらないで　ください。

Please don't take pictures here.
#
3) [ない-form なければ　なりません] I must...

This expression means something has to be done regardless of the will of the actor. Note that this doesn't have a negative meaning.

くすりを　のまなければ　なりません。 I must take medicine.
#
4) ない-form [なくても　いい　です] need not ...

This pattern indicates that the described action does not have to be done.

あした　こなくても　いい　です。 You don't have to come tomorrow.
#
5) までに

The point in time indicated by までに is the time limit by which an action is to be done.

かいぎは　５じまでに　おわります。 The meeting will be over by five.

どようびまでに　ほんを　かえさなければ　なりません。I must return the book by Saturday.
