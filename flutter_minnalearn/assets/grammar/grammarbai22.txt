
1) Noun modification

You learned how to modify nouns in Lesson 2 and Lesson 8.

ミラーさんの　うち Mr. Miller's house (L. 2)

あたらしい　うち a new house (L. 8)

きれいな　うち a beautiful house (L. 8)

In Japanese, whatever modifies a word, whether it's a word or a sentence, it always comes before the word to be modified. Here you learn another way to modify nouns.
#
2.1) Noun modification by sentences

Sentences which modify a noun are put in the plain form. In な-adjective sentences, ~ だ becomes ~な. In noun sentences ~だ becomes ~の.
#
2.2) Nouns can be picked out of a sentence and be modified by it.

わたしは　せんしゅう　えいがを　みました → わたしが　せんしゅう　みた えいが

I saw a movie last week → the movie that I saw last week

ワンさんは　びょういんで　はたらいて　います → ワンさんが　はたらいて　いる　びょういん

Mr. Wang works at a hospital → the hospital where Mr. Wang works

In that case, を, で and に dissappear.
#
2.3) The noun modified by a sentence ("the house where Mr. Miller lived" in the example sentences below) can be used in various parts of a sentence.
#
3) が

When a sentence modifies a noun, the subject in the sentence is indicated by が.

みらさんは　ケーキを　つくりました。

Mr. Miller baked a cake.

→

これは　みらさんが　つくった　ケーキ　です。

This is the cake which Mr. Miller baked.

カリナさんが　かいた　えが　すき　です。

I like the picture that Ms. Karina drew.
#
4) dictionary form じかん/やくそく/ようじ

When expressing the time for doing some activity, put the dictionary form of the action before じかん.

あさごはんを　たべる　じかんが　ありません。

I have no time to eat breakfast.

You can also describe the content of an appointment, etc., by putting the dictionary form of that action before やくそく, etc.

ともだちと　えいがを　みる　やくそくが　あります。

I have an arrangement to see a movie with a friend of mine.

きょうは　しやくしょへ　いく　ようじが　あります。

I have something to do at the city hall today.
