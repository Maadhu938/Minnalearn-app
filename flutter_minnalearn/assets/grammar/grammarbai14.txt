
1) て-form(Imperative)

The verb form which ends with て or で works as imperative. How to make the て-form of a verb depends on which group the verb belongs to as explained below. This separation into groups will be highly relevant in later chapters as well.
#
1.1) Group I verbs

The last sound of the ます-form is that of the い-line. Depending on the last sound of the ます-form, the て-form of the verbs of this group is made as shown in the table. Note that theて-form of the verb いきます, いって, is an exception.
#
1.2) Group II verbs

In most of the verbs of this group, the last sound of the ます-form is that of the え-line, but in some verbs the last sound of the ます-form is that of the い-line.
#
1.3) Group III verbs

Group III consist only of きます, します and verbs that are variations of these two.
#
2) Vて-form います

This sentence pattern indicates that a certain action or motion is in progress.

ミラーさんは　いま　でんわを　かきて　います。

Mr. Miller is making a phone call now.

いま　あめが　ふって　いますか。

Is it raining now?
#
3) Vます-form ましょうか - Shall I...?

This expression is used when the speaker is offering to do something for the listener.

あしたも　きましょうか。

Shall I come tomorrow, too?
#
4) S が S - ..., but...

しつれいですが、おなまえは？

Excuse me, but may I have your name?

すみませんが、しおを　とって　ください。

Please pass me the salt.
#
5) NがN

When describing a natural phenomenon, the subject is indicated by が.

あめが　ふって　います。

It is raining.
