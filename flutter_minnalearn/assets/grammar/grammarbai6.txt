
1) Nを V(transitive)

をis used to indicate the direct object of a transitive verb.

ジュースを　のみます。I drink juice.

[Note] をand お are pronounced the same. The former is used only in writing the particle .

2) Nを　します

The words used as the objects of the verb しますcover a fairly wide range. します means that the action denoted by the noun is performed.

l) to“play" sports or games

サッカーを　します play football

トランプを　します play cards

II) to "hold" gatherings

パーティーを　します give a party

かいぎを　します hold a meeting

III) to "do" something

しゅくだいを　します do homework

しごとを　します do one's work
#
3) なにを　しますか

This is a question to ask what someone does.

げつようびは　なにを　しますか。 What will you do on Monday?

・・きょうとへ　いきます。I'll go to Kyoto.
#
4) なんand なに

Both なん and なに mean“what."

I) なんis used in the following cases.

When it precedes a word whose first mora is either in the た, だ or な-row.

それは　なんですか。What is that?

なんの　ほんですか。What is the book about?

ねるまえに、なんと　いいますか。What do you say before going to bed?

When it is followed by a counter suffix or the like.

テレサちゃんは　なんさい　ですか。How old is Teresa?

II) なに is used in all other cases.

なにを　かいますか。What will you buy?
#
5) N(place)でV

When added after a noun denoting a place, で indicates the place where an action occurs.

えきで　しんぶんを　かいます。 I buy the newspaper at the station.
#
6) Vませんか

When you want to invite someone to do something, this expression is used.

いっしょに　きょうとへ　いきませんか。 Won't you come to Kyoto with us?
#
7) Vましょう

This expression is used when a speaker is inviting the listener to do something with the speaker. It is also used when responding positively to an invitation.

ちょっと　やすみましょう。Let's have a break.

いっしょに　ひるごはんを　たべませんか。Won't you have lunch with me?

…ええ、たべましょう。Yes, let's eat.

[Note] An invitation using Vませんかshows more consideration to the listener's will than that using Vましょう.
